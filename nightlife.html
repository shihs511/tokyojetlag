Safety & Etiquette Tips

Cover Charges

Most clubs charge ¥2,000-5,000 cover (includes one drink). Live houses ¥2,000-3,500. Bars and karaoke charge hourly (¥300-800/hour). Always ask before sitting down in small bars.

Last Train Times

Last train is 11:45pm-12:30am on most lines. After that: taxis, night buses, or stay out until first train at 5:00am. Plan your exit strategy. Taxis are pricey after midnight.

Tipping Culture

NO TIPPING in Tokyo. Ever. Not in bars, not in clubs, not in restaurants. Servers refuse it. Tips are included in prices. Don't be the tourist who tries.

Drink Prices

Bars: ¥500-1,500/drink. Golden Gai: ¥1,000-3,000. High-end cocktail bars: ¥1,500-3,000. Clubs: drinks ¥1,000-2,000 (expensive). Golden Rule: have cash, ATMs exist everywhere.

Dress Code

Casual fine for most bars, Shimokitazawa, live houses. Clubs: smart casual (no flip-flops, athletic wear). High-end bars: business casual. When in doubt, dress nice.

Language & Manners

English is limited outside major tourist areas. Download Google Translate. Respect personal space. No smoking indoors (designated areas only). Don't yell. Bartenders are artists, treat them with respect.

Safety

Tokyo is extremely safe. Walk anywhere, anytime. Don't flash valuables. Keep your phone close. Drunk harassment is rare but avoid empty streets alone drunk. Stay with friends if possible.

Golden Gai Etiquette

Golden Gai bars are tiny and intimate. Don't be loud. Bartenders are telling stories. Join the conversation. Respect locals. Don't take photos. Tip is included—just be cool.

Tokyo Nightlife Timeline

12:00am
Midnight Rush
Clubs are hitting stride. WOMB, Contact, WARP Shinjuku pumping. Most bars still full. Golden Gai in full swing. Karaoke rooms packed with groups.
1:00am
Peak Energy
Clubs at maximum capacity. Dancefloors packed. Bars transition from solo drinkers to groups. Some restaurants shutting down, more clubs opening. Street vendors appear.
2:00am
The Late Crowd
Serious night owls emerge. Club energy intensifying. Golden Gai gets rowdier. Karaoke still full. First-train crew shows up for last round. Streets busy with night workers.
3:00am
The Commitment Zone
You're staying up or you're not. Clubs at full throttle. Some venues hitting closing time. Ramen shops opening for hungry drinkers. Shimokitazawa still going. Nakano bars deep into night.
4:00am
Hardcore Hours
Only the committed remain. Top clubs still going. After-parties forming. All-night bars filling up for dawn drinkers. Night shift workers, hospital staff, insomniacs gather.
5:00am
First Train Hour
Last clubs close. First train arrives—exodus to outer wards. Some venues do after-parties. All-night ramen packed. City transitions to morning shift workers. Clean-up crews begin.
6:00am
Sunrise Shift
Most clubs officially closed. Last drunk people heading home. Early bars open for salarymen. Coffee shops filling up. Night becomes day. Cycle begins again.
